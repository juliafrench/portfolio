After receiving my BA at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, I moved to Chicago and began my career at Showtime Networks, working as promotions coordinator for premium cable services. I then went to work as a copywriter for a startup firm called VideOcart, a B2C startup that designed, installed, and operated a nationwide system of 22,000 wireless PDAs on shopping carts.

After earning an MA in Writing at DePaul University and relocating to Milwaukee, I taught writing and composition at the University of Milwaukee-Wisconsin, Concordia University, and Milwaukee Area Technical College.

I then fulfilled a lifelong dream and moved to Anchorage, Alaska, in 1995. I worked in a variety of editorial roles: I wrote policies and procedures for the Municipality of Anchorage, Department of Public Works; I wrote Invitations to Bid and RFPs for construction projects for the Alaska Court System Project Office; taught technical writing and research at the University of Alaska; and served as an affiliate editor for the Alaska Quarterly Review, an award-winning literary journal.

In 2000, I relocated to Seattle, Washington, and worked as a technical editor for AT&T Wireless for five years, and then as a contract technical writer for Boeing. I currently do editorial contract work for a variety of freelance clients, including Humanities Software.
